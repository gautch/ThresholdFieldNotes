2026.02.12 23:47:23 EST PERIMETER-W INCIDENT

At precisely 23:47:23 EST, PERIMETER-W motion sensors triggered a Level 3 alert indicating significant movement along the western property boundary. Initial review of the footage showed movement consistent with a large terrestrial animals crossing the monitored zone.

Enhanced analysis revealed several anomalies inconsistent with known regional wildlife:

Primary Observation: Subjects moved through frame in approximately 4.2 seconds, covering an estimated 18 feet. Gait analysis indicates bipedal locomotion with stride length of 4.5-5 feet. Height estimate based on reference markers: 6'4" to 7'2".
FRAME 001 / 20:02:23 EST - Initial detection at western boundary - East facing camera
FRAME 018 / 20:45:07 EST - Subject in mid-transit - West facing camera
FRAME 034 / 23:47:27 EST - Potentially Resident Researcher out on a walk - North facing camera

Cross-reference with regional wildlife databases produced no matches. Black bear (Ursus americanus) was initially considered, however:

  • → Movement pattern shows consistent bipedal locomotion, not typical bear behavior
  • → Stride length exceeds documented black bear parameters
  • → No corresponding tracks found during daylight investigation of area
  • → Ambient temperature (28°F) makes extended bipedal movement by bear highly unlikely

Audio analysis detected no vocalizations. Thermal imaging (retrospective analysis) shows heat signature consistent with large mammal, though thermal resolution at range limits detailed analysis.

Investigation Notes:
  • Ground survey conducted 02/13 at 08:00 EST - no physical evidence recovered
  • Camera system verified operational, no equipment malfunction detected
  • Cross-referenced with previous incidents: similar event logged 01/28 at STATION-X (offline)
  • Motion detection sensitivity increased for PERIMETER-W zone
  • Local wildlife services notified as protocol requires
  • Follow-up observation schedule implemented

This incident has been classified as unexplained pending further investigation. All footage has been archived and is available for review by qualified observers. Community reports of similar observations in the area are encouraged.
